Our Story

We started Hello Learners because we kept seeing the same thing.

Bright kids. Worried parents. And a gap that nobody seemed to be filling. Every session at Hello Learners exists because of what we kept seeing in the clinic — and what was clearly missing everywhere else.

"Reading difficulties need more than curriculum. They need someone who understands how language works."

Why we exist

The missing piece was always language.

When Darcie was seeing children at Hello Kids Therapy Hub for speech and language support, something kept appearing: reading difficulties. Not occasionally. Consistently. And always with parents who'd already tried everything they knew to try.

What we noticed was this — the children who were struggling most weren't lacking intelligence or effort. Something specific was going wrong in how they were connecting the sounds in words to the letters on the page. And once we understood what was wrong, we knew exactly how to help.

Reading isn't just about practice or persistence. It's about how your brain matches spoken sounds to written symbols. When that system isn't working smoothly, more practice doesn't fix it. You need someone who understands the mechanism — and knows how to rebuild it.

That's why we built Hello Learners.

Speech Pathologist & Director

Darcie

Darcie founded Hello Kids Therapy Hub because she kept seeing the same pattern: children with significant reading difficulties who hadn't been referred to anyone who could actually help. She runs Hello Learners because she's seen the difference it makes when a child gets the right kind of support, at the right time.

Every clinician is Speech Pathology Australia registered and trained in structured literacy. When your child is in a Hello Learners session, a qualified speech pathologist is in the room. That matters — because reading difficulties are language difficulties, and that's what we're trained to understand.

What we believe

The principles Hello Learners is built around.

favorite

Struggling with reading is almost never about effort.

The children who come to Hello Learners have been trying. They know something's wrong. The problem isn't motivation — it's that they're working with a system that isn't working. That's something we can actually fix.

schedule

You shouldn't have to wait for a diagnosis to get help.

If you've noticed your child is struggling, that's enough. You don't need a GP referral or a formal diagnosis. We'll assess them, tell you what we see, and you can decide from there.

diversity_3

Small groups change what's possible.

When a child realises the other kids in the room are struggling with the same thing, something shifts. The shame decreases. The effort feels lighter. That's not incidental — it's core to how learning happens.
