The program

A program where kids make real progress — and feel good doing it.

Hello Learners

Our specialist after-school literacy program for primary school children who need extra support with reading, writing and spelling. Sessions run in small groups of 2–4 children, led by qualified speech pathologists who know how to make progress feel possible — and genuinely enjoyable.

Children work on real skills in a setting where they quickly realise something important: the other kids in the room are finding it hard too. That shift alone changes everything.

Reading is a language skill. Which means when it breaks down, the root cause is nearly always in how the brain processes language — not in how hard your child is working. Speech pathology is the profession that specialises in exactly this.

That's why our results tend to go further than tutoring.

FAQs

Questions we hear all the time

Does my child need a diagnosis to join?
No. Many families come to us without any formal diagnosis — just a feeling that something isn't quite right. We assess each child before the term starts so we understand exactly how to support them in the group.
Why a speech pathologist — isn't this a tutor's job?
Reading is fundamentally a language skill — it requires the brain to map the sound structure of spoken language onto written symbols. When that process breaks down, the root cause is almost always in language processing. Speech pathology is the profession specifically trained to understand and treat language processing difficulties. That's why our results tend to go further than tutoring.
How long before we see results?
Most families notice meaningful change within the first term — in both skills and confidence. We track progress throughout and share that with you, so you're never left wondering how things are going.
What if my child is shy or anxious about joining a group?
This comes up a lot. Groups of 2–4 children are small enough that most children settle quickly — especially once they realise the other kids are working through the same things. We're experienced at helping anxious children feel safe in the room.
Is Hello Learners covered by Medicare or NDIS?
Some of our services are eligible for Medicare rebates under a GP-referred care plan, and we are NDIS registered. We'll help you understand what funding may apply during your first conversation with us.
When does the next term start?
Hello Learners runs each school term. Get in touch and we'll let you know when enrolments open — spots are limited so it's worth reaching out early.
